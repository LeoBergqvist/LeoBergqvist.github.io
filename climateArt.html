ClimateART

Unity, C#, Git

Description: An educational experience for elementary school students about emissions from different energy sources. Link:

Overview

ClimateART was an interactive, collaborative platform that allowed users to explore a shared virtual planet, visualize power plants and CO₂ emissions, and leave interactive “footprints” for others to build upon. It was inspired by the theme “The future of learning about STEM” and aligned with Swedish curriculum goals on energy and environmental impact. The project was also timely with the COP26 climate conference.

Development Timeline

  • Weeks 1–2: The team brainstormed and defined the scope.
  • Weeks 3–5: Three one-week sprints focused on product development.
  • Week 6: The team finalized integration and presented the project to the public.

Core Goals

  • Enable interactive, cross-platform collaboration.
  • Create visually engaging and appealing graphics.
  • Provide an effective learning experience about climate issues.

Technical Implementation

MVP: The team used Node.js with Socket.io for platform connections, combining a Unity 3D world with a browser-based 2D map (Leaflet.js). Users could tap the map to place trees, which appeared in the 3D world.

Enhancements:

  • Interaction: Users could select from multiple objects, choose aliases, and interact via a mobile-responsive Onsen UI interface.
  • Graphics: A detailed 3D world and models were created in Blender and imported into Unity; shaders for water and cables were scripted.
  • Logic: A CO₂ emission graph and energy source bar were displayed in Unity; a day/night cycle and environmental effects like fog appeared when emissions rose too high.

User Testing & Deployment

The project was deployed on a Raspberry Pi, and participants joined by scanning a QR code on their own devices. It was demonstrated to the public during open house events, where users tested the system and explored the virtual world.

Challenges & Learnings

  • Overcoming challenges to make the visual experience interactive and engaging required creative problem-solving and iterative user feedback.
  • Designing a clear and useful user interface for end users is essential to ensure ease of navigation and engagement.
  • Adding world effects such as weather cycles and increasing smoke from pollution significantly enhances user immersion in the virtual environment.

Future Potential

The project served as a proof of concept with strong potential for expansion in education, collaboration, and environmental exploration.
