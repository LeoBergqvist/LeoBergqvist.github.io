Arthello

Unity, C#

Description: An AR version of the classic game Othello / reversi made in the game engine Unity. Link:

Overview

ARthello was an augmented reality adaptation of the Othello board game, developed in Unity with Vuforia for image-based tracking. Built for Android, it aimed to help novice players learn the game compared to traditional 2D versions, using reflective design principles to assess its affordances and limitations.

Goals

  • Explore how AR can enhance learning and engagement in traditional board games.
  • Evaluate AR’s strengths and limitations against a 2D screen-based Othello.
  • Investigate design opportunities using reflective design principles.

Development

  • Created in Unity for Android, with C# scripting and the Vuforia AR plugin.
  • Used printed or digital image targets to project a virtual board in real space.
  • Integrated realistic visuals, haptic vibration feedback, and recorded sound effects.

Evaluation Method

  • In-house playtesting, think-aloud sessions, and reflective design analysis due to pandemic restrictions.
  • Focused on uncovering design limitations and rethinking interaction possibilities.
  • Self-run focus group discussions to assess novice player training potential.

Key Findings

  • AR gameplay encouraged physical engagement, perspective-shifting, and strategic exploration.
  • Rotating or repositioning the board improved motivation and situational awareness.
  • Sound and vibration added realism and reinforced feedback, but risked biasing decisions.
  • Limitations included fatigue, accessibility barriers, rendering issues, and lack of deep strategic assistance.

Key Learnings

  • Applied a design thinking process—defining problems, exploring novice player needs, and refining ideas through prototyping and reflective evaluation.
  • Gained hands-on experience with Unity and C#, balancing technical feasibility with engaging gameplay.
  • Learned to leverage Vuforia for stable AR image tracking, optimizing for lighting, positioning, and immersion.
  • Designed physical interactions—rotating and moving around the board—to boost engagement and strategic insight, while managing accessibility and comfort.
  • Used multimodal feedback (sound, haptics, visuals) to enhance realism, revealing how sensory cues can subtly shape decision-making.
  • Applied reflective design to turn limitations into opportunities, inspiring future ideas like AI opponents, alternative inputs, and shared AR gameplay.
